# Feature Spec: Payment Processing
_Status: Draft | Priority: High | Author: Winston + Havee_

---

## Summary
The platform handles all payment end-to-end — from dispatch fee collection through final job settlement. Revenue is generated at two points: upfront at dispatch and at job close. Customers see clean flat fees; internally fees are percentage-based and scale with job size.

---

## Revenue Points

### 1. Dispatch Fee (upfront)
- Charged immediately when customer submits a request
- Flat fee varies by urgency tier:
  - Emergency: ~$50
  - Standard/Scheduled: lower rate (TBD — test and optimize)
- Pure platform revenue — collected before any vendor sees the job
- Non-refundable (covers routing, admin, and guarantee costs)

### 2. Service Fee at Close (percentage, displayed as flat)
- Charged when customer approves job completion
- **Customer-facing:** displayed as a clean flat dollar amount (e.g. "$35 service fee")
- **Internally:** calculated as a percentage of job total — scales with job size automatically
- Tiered flat fee display by job size (e.g. sub-$500 = one fee tier, $500+ = another)
- Platform processes full payment, remits to vendor minus service fee

---

## Payment Flow

```
CUSTOMER SUBMITS → DISPATCH FEE CHARGED
       ↓
VENDOR SUBMITS QUOTE → CUSTOMER REVIEWS & APPROVES
       ↓
CARD AUTHORIZED (full job amount + service fee held)
       ↓
WORK COMPLETED → VENDOR MARKS DONE
       ↓
CUSTOMER APPROVES → CARD CAPTURED
       ↓
PLATFORM REMITS TO VENDOR (job total minus service fee)
```

### Key Principle: Card held at quote approval, captured only at customer sign-off
- Dispatch fee: charged immediately at submission
- Job total + service fee: authorized at quote approval, captured at customer approval
- Customers know their card won't be fully charged until they're satisfied
- Builds trust, reduces disputes

---

## Vendor Quoting

- After accepting a job, vendor reviews full request details (description, photos, urgency)
- Vendor submits a quote before any work begins
- Customer receives quote notification and must approve before vendor proceeds
- Quote approval triggers card authorization
- No work should begin without customer-approved quote (platform policy)

## Dispute Resolution

### Philosophy
We arbitrate. Our guarantee is our brand. On a small island, reputation travels fast — how we handle problems is what defines us.

### Process
1. Customer flags an issue after job "completion"
2. Platform requests evidence from both sides:
   - Customer: description of issue, photos
   - Vendor: completion notes, completion photos
3. Platform (Winston/Nick) reviews evidence and makes a final decision
4. Decision is communicated to both parties

### When the Customer Wins
- **No additional charge to the customer** — ever
- Platform automatically dispatches the next available tier vendor to fix the issue
- **Platform guarantees payment to the remediation vendor** out of platform revenue
- Original vendor may be penalized (tier demotion, warning, or removal depending on severity)

### When the Vendor Wins
- Customer dispute is closed, payment released to vendor
- Customer account noted internally

### The Risk We Accept
- We absorb the cost of remediation jobs
- This is a calculated business risk — the brand loyalty and word-of-mouth generated by making customers whole outweighs the cost
- Tracked internally as a KPI: dispute rate, remediation cost per month, resolution time

---

## Refund Policy (Simple Cases)
For straightforward situations, automatic resolution before arbitration:
- Job never started (vendor accepted but no-showed) → full refund including dispatch fee
- Job cancelled by customer before vendor arrives → dispatch fee retained, job total refunded
- All other disputes → full arbitration process

## Payment Infrastructure Notes (for Nick)
- Stripe is the recommended processor — supports authorize/capture flow, marketplace payouts, and dispute handling natively
- Stripe Connect for vendor payouts — vendors onboard once, platform remits automatically
- Consider Stripe's built-in dispute/chargeback tools as a first layer before manual arbitration
- Dispatch fee should be a separate charge line item, clearly labeled
- All transactions logged in admin dashboard with full audit trail

---

## Admin Dashboard Requirements
- View all transactions (pending, authorized, captured, refunded)
- Manually trigger refunds
- View dispute queue
- Track platform revenue by fee type (dispatch fees vs. service fees)
- Monthly revenue summary
- Dispute rate and remediation cost KPIs
